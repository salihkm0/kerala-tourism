Wayanad

Wayanad, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a pristine and enchanting destination that captivates visitors with its lush green landscapes, mist-covered hills, and rich biodiversity. This idyllic region is known for its verdant tea and spice plantations, dense forests, and numerous wildlife sanctuaries. The heart of Wayanad, Kalpetta, serves as a gateway to its scenic wonders, including the iconic Soochipara Falls, Banasura Sagar Dam, and the ancient Edakkal Caves adorned with prehistoric petroglyphs. Nature enthusiasts can explore the wildlife at Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, home to elephants, tigers, and a variety of bird species. With its refreshing climate, stunning natural beauty, and a harmonious blend of adventure and tranquility, Wayanad offers a unique and immersive experience for those seeking an escape into the lap of Kerala's Western Ghats.

Top Places to visit

Edakkal Caves

Nestled amidst the Ambukuthi Hills, Edakkal Caves stand as a testament to Wayanad's historical richness. These ancient caves are renowned for their rock engravings, dating back to the Neolithic Age, depicting scenes of human and animal figures. Accessible through a moderate trek, the caves offer not just a journey into the past but also panoramic views of the lush Western Ghats from the summit. The unique experience of exploring these natural rock formations, coupled with their archaeological significance, makes Edakkal Caves a compelling attraction for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Banasura Sagar Dam

Set against the breathtaking backdrop of the Banasura Hills, Banasura Sagar Dam is a monumental earth dam and the largest of its kind in India. The dam encompasses the vast Banasura Sagar reservoir, providing a serene environment surrounded by rolling hills and lush landscapes. Boating on the reservoir is a popular activity, allowing visitors to absorb the scenic beauty and marvel at the submerged islands. The dam is not only a testament to engineering but also a scenic spot that invites travelers to unwind in the lap of nature.

Pookode Lake

Tucked away amid emerald-green hills, Pookode Lake is a picturesque freshwater lake that enchants visitors with its serene ambiance. Surrounded by thick forests and lush greenery, the lake offers boating facilities, providing a tranquil experience on its pristine waters. The scenic walkways along the lake and the abundance of avian life add to the charm, making Pookode Lake a delightful destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat in the heart of Wayanad's natural splendor.

Soochipara Falls

Soochipara Falls, also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls, is a captivating three-tiered waterfall surrounded by dense forests and steep cliffs. The falls cascade from a height, creating a mesmerizing spectacle amidst the lush greenery. A short trek through the forest leads to the base of the falls, offering an exhilarating experience and a natural pool where visitors can enjoy a refreshing dip. The combination of adventure, scenic beauty, and the soothing sound of cascading water makes Soochipara Falls a favorite destination for nature enthusiasts and thrill-seekers alike.
