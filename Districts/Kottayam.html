Kottayam

Nestled in the heart of Kerala, Kottayam is a culturally vibrant district known for its scenic backwaters, historic landmarks, and literary significance. Serving as a gateway to the serene backwaters of Kumarakom and Vaikom, the city offers houseboat cruises that showcase the unique lifestyle along the waterways. Renowned as the "City of Letters," Kottayam boasts a prominent role in the publishing industry, with numerous printing and publishing houses contributing to the region's rich literary and educational heritage. The cityscape features colonial-era churches, temples, and historic buildings, while landmarks like the Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple and Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple add cultural depth. Surrounded by lush landscapes, Kottayam also presents attractions like Vembanad Lake, Illikkal Kallu, and Kumarakom's bird sanctuary. With a rich tapestry of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and literary prominence, Kottayam stands as a captivating destination in Kerala.

Top Places to visit

Kumarakom Backwaters

Kumarakom, nestled on the banks of Vembanad Lake, offers a mesmerizing experience through its serene backwaters. The intricate network of canals, lagoons, and lakes makes Kumarakom an ideal destination for houseboat cruises. Visitors can glide through the calm waters, surrounded by lush greenery and quaint villages, witnessing the unique lifestyle of the region. The backwaters of Kumarakom provide a tranquil escape, allowing guests to unwind amidst nature's beauty and enjoy the peaceful ambiance that defines this scenic destination.

Vembanad Lake

As one of the largest lakes in India and a centerpiece of the Kottayam district, Vembanad Lake is a majestic waterbody that contributes to the charm of Kumarakom. The lake spans across several districts and offers stunning views of its expansive waters against the backdrop of the Western Ghats. Boating on Vembanad Lake allows visitors to soak in the panoramic landscapes, witness vibrant birdlife, and experience the cultural richness of the surrounding villages, making it a quintessential part of the Kumarakom backwaters.

Illikkal Kallu

For adventure enthusiasts, Illikkal Kallu presents a thrilling trekking destination near Kottayam. The name translates to "Moonrock," and the peak offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the surrounding landscapes. The challenging trek takes visitors through lush greenery and rocky terrains, culminating in a rewarding vista at the summit. Illikkal Kallu is not only a haven for trekking but also a serene spot to connect with nature and appreciate the beauty of the Kerala highlands.

Kottayam Bird Sanctuary

Situated on the banks of Vembanad Lake, the Kottayam Bird Sanctuary, also known as the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to a diverse range of migratory and resident bird species, including herons, egrets, kingfishers, and more. Visitors can explore the sanctuary through boat rides, witnessing the vibrant avian life amidst the serene surroundings of the backwaters. The tranquil ambiance and the harmonious coexistence of nature and wildlife make the bird sanctuary an essential stop for those seeking a peaceful encounter with Kerala's natural beauty.
