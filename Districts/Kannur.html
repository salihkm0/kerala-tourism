Kannur

Kannur, located on the northern Malabar Coast of Kerala, India, is a captivating destination that seamlessly blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Renowned for its pristine beaches, historic forts, and vibrant folk arts, Kannur offers a diverse array of experiences. The St. Angelo Fort, with its centuries-old charm and strategic location, narrates tales of colonial influence. The sandy shores of Payyambalam Beach and the serene atmosphere of Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach beckon beach enthusiasts. Kannur is also celebrated for its traditional art forms like Theyyam, a unique dance ritual showcasing vibrant costumes and intricate rituals. The Arakkal Museum, housed in the Arakkal Palace, offers insights into the history of the region's only Muslim royal family. With its lively markets, rich cultural heritage, and natural wonders, Kannur stands as a multifaceted gem on Kerala's coastal landscape.

Top Places to visit

St. Angelo Fort

Overlooking the Arabian Sea, St. Angelo Fort in Kannur is a historic marvel that transports visitors to a bygone era. Originally constructed by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the British, the fort stands as a testament to the colonial struggles for dominance in the region. The well-preserved structure boasts distinctive architecture, with its angular bastions and a scenic location near Moppila Bay. Visitors can explore the ramparts, dungeons, and a lighthouse, offering panoramic views of the coastline. St. Angelo Fort not only narrates tales of military strategy but also provides a serene environment for those seeking a blend of history and scenic beauty.

Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach

Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach, extending for about 4 kilometers along the Arabian Sea, is a unique destination that allows visitors to drive along the shoreline. Its gently sloping sandy shores create a natural pathway for vehicles, offering a thrilling and picturesque drive. The beach, lined with coconut groves, provides a laid-back atmosphere for relaxation and recreation. The opportunity to enjoy both the thrill of driving and the soothing coastal ambiance makes Muzhappilangad a popular and distinctive destination for beachgoers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Theyyam Performances

Theyyam, a traditional ritualistic art form of North Malabar, comes alive in Kannur with its vibrant performances and intricate rituals. This unique dance form involves elaborate costumes, mesmerizing makeup, and intense drumming, depicting a divine connection with deities. Various temples in the region host Theyyam performances during specific times of the year, attracting both locals and tourists. The colorful and ritualistic nature of Theyyam showcases the rich cultural heritage of Kannur and provides a captivating experience for those interested in traditional art and folklore.

Arakkal Museum

Housed in the historic Arakkal Palace, the Arakkal Museum is a cultural gem that sheds light on the history of the region's only Muslim royal family. The museum displays artifacts, relics, and personal belongings of the Arakkal family, offering insights into their royal lifestyle and contributions. The palace itself is an architectural marvel, and the museum provides a captivating journey through time, narrating the tales of the Arakkal rulers. The exhibits include weaponry, artifacts, and portraits, creating a captivating narrative of Kannur's unique historical tapestry.
