Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a vibrant city on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, India, that seamlessly blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Renowned for its rich maritime heritage, Kozhikode was a significant trading hub for spices and textiles in ancient times. The city's historic sites, such as the centuries-old Mananchira Square and the iconic Kappad Beach, where Vasco da Gama first set foot in India, offer glimpses into its storied past. The bustling markets, like the centuries-old Sweet Meat Street, showcase the city's culinary delights, with a variety of traditional snacks and sweets. Kozhikode is also a gateway to the serene backwaters of Kallai River and the lush landscapes of Wayanad. With its blend of historical landmarks, cultural richness, and scenic beauty, Kozhikode provides an immersive experience for travelers exploring the unique charm of Kerala's Malabar region.

Top Places to visit

Kappad Beach

Kappad Beach, steeped in historical significance, beckons visitors with its tranquil shores along the Arabian Sea. This idyllic beach is renowned as the place where Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama first landed in India in 1498. The beach offers not only a serene escape but also a glimpse into the past, with a small stone monument commemorating the historic landing. Surrounded by coconut groves and featuring golden sands, Kappad Beach is an inviting destination for those seeking both a serene coastal retreat and a connection to the region's maritime history.

Mananchira Square

At the heart of Kozhikode lies the picturesque Mananchira Square, a meticulously landscaped park surrounding a large pond. The square serves as a central hub for cultural events and leisurely strolls, offering a respite from the city's hustle and bustle. The space is adorned with sculptures, a musical fountain, and the historic Mananchira Palace, creating a harmonious blend of greenery and architectural elegance. Locals and tourists alike frequent this vibrant square, making it a dynamic focal point that encapsulates the essence of Kozhikode's cultural and historical heritage.

Sweet Meat Street (Mittayi Theruvu):

For a culinary adventure, Sweet Meat Street, or Mittayi Theruvu, is a vibrant and aromatic stretch in Kozhikode. Lined with shops offering an array of traditional snacks, sweets, and savory treats, the street is a haven for food enthusiasts. From the famous Kozhikodan halwa to banana chips and other regional delicacies, Sweet Meat Street tantalizes the taste buds with the flavors of Kerala's culinary traditions. The lively atmosphere and the irresistible aroma of freshly prepared snacks make it a must-visit destination for those looking to savor the local cuisine.

Kozhikode Beach

Kozhikode Beach, along the Malabar Coast, is a captivating stretch of shoreline where the Arabian Sea gently meets the land. Ideal for leisurely walks, the beachfront offers a serene ambiance and stunning views of the sea. The bustling activity of local vendors, fishermen, and the iconic old lighthouse add to the charm. Sunset strolls, the sound of crashing waves, and the cool sea breeze make Kozhikode Beach a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, providing a perfect setting to unwind and enjoy the coastal beauty.
